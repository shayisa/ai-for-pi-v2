# Quick Reference: Voice, Tone & Stylistic Flavor Guide for Newsletter AI

## QUICK LOOKUP: PERSONA SELECTION

**Choose one primary persona + optional secondary**

| Persona | Best For | Opening Typically Sounds Like |
|---------|----------|------------------------------|
| **Expert Authority** | Industry news, data journalism, analysis | "Our research with 50+ companies found..." or "After deep investigation, we discovered..." |
| **Peer/Friend** | Personal growth, culture, lifestyle | "So I was completely wrong about..." or "You know that feeling when..." |
| **Guide/Mentor** | How-to, education, onboarding | "Let me walk you through how this works..." or "Here's what you need before deciding..." |
| **Curator** | News aggregation, culture, discovery | "Out of 500 articles, these 3 matter..." or "Found something worth your time..." |
| **Provocateur** | Opinion, commentary, business takes | "Everyone's wrong about this..." or "Here's what nobody wants to say..." |
| **Storyteller** | Features, narrative essays, case studies | "Sarah didn't expect what happened next..." or "The moment changed everything..." |

## QUICK LOOKUP: TONE SELECTION

**Choose 1-2 primary tones**

| Tone | Sounds Like | When To Use | Example Opening |
|------|------------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Warm** | Friendly, accepting, celebratory | Community, support, onboarding | "Welcome! We're so glad you're here because..." |
| **Confident** | Sure, direct, no hedging | Business, finance, leadership | "Here's what works. We've tested it with..." |
| **Witty** | Clever, funny, insider jokes | News, culture, lifestyle | "This tool is impossibly good. Here's why..." |
| **Empathetic** | Understanding, validating, supportive | Wellness, difficult topics, support | "I know this is hard. You're not alone because..." |
| **Analytical** | Thoughtful, intellectual, nuanced | Business, science, policy | "On the surface this seems like X, but actually..." |
| **Urgent** | Fast-paced, FOMO, action-focused | Breaking news, product launches | "This changes everything. Here's why you need to know..." |
| **Introspective** | Reflective, questioning, contemplative | Essays, personal development | "I've been thinking about this all week..." |
| **Serious** | Formal, grave, respectful gravity | Crisis, investigative, policy | "This matter demands your attention because..." |

## QUICK LOOKUP: STYLISTIC FLAVOR

**Choose 1 primary + 1 optional secondary**

| Flavor | Looks Like | When To Use | Structure |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Conversational** | "you," "I," contractions, spoken rhythm | Personal, friendly content | Short sentences, natural rhythm, ellipses for pauses |
| **Scannable** | Headers, bullets, bold text, white space | Daily news, busy audiences | Max 2-3 sentences per paragraph, clear hierarchy |
| **Narrative** | Scene-setting, characters, tension arc | Stories, features, case studies | Opens with scene/character, builds tension, payoff |
| **Data-Driven** | Numbers prominent, charts/graphs, statistics | Finance, business, analysis | Numbers bolded/highlighted, comparisons, trends |
| **Journalistic** | Quotes, reporting, sources, specific details | Features, news, investigation | Direct quotes, attribution, scene details, context |
| **Lyrical** | Metaphors, beautiful language, sensory details | Essays, brand storytelling, culture | Rich vocabulary, figurative language, rhythm |
| **Minimalist** | Short sentences, one idea each, spare | Urgent, certain tech content | Subject-verb-object, white space significant |
| **Irreverent** | Breaking rules, sarcasm, profanity, sassy | Opinion, provocative content | Exclamation marks, dashes, slang, "says what others won't" |
| **Visual/Graphic** | Images, icons, color-coded sections, infographics | Design, product updates, visual storytelling | Generous white space, large typography, icon-driven |

## PLATFORM-SPECIFIC QUICK COMBOS

### Daily News Newsletters (Morning Brew, TLDR)
```
Persona: Curator + Friend
Tone: Witty + Direct + Energetic
Flavor: Scannable + Conversational

Key: Personality + Speed. Make boring news fun.

Example Opening:
"☕ Your briefing is ready

The story nobody's talking about yet.
Why it matters: [1-2 sentence explanation]
The data: [Key numbers highlighted]"
```

### Long-Form Essays (Wait But Why, The Atlantic)
```
Persona: Storyteller + Expert Authority
Tone: Contemplative + Thoughtful
Flavor: Narrative + Introspective

Key: Go deep. Take readers on a journey.

Example Opening:
"Sarah stood in the office at 3 AM, re-reading the email for the third time.
That's when she realized something that nobody talks about: ..."
```

### Business Newsletters (Lenny's, The Generalist)
```
Persona: Expert Authority + Peer
Tone: Confident + Analytical
Flavor: Data-Driven + Journalistic

Key: Original research. Actionable insights. "In the mix" perspective.

Example Opening:
"We spent 6 months interviewing product leaders.
Here's what we learned that contradicts conventional wisdom..."
```

### Culture/Lifestyle (Blackbird Spyplane, Dense Discovery)
```
Persona: Curator + Friend + Provocateur
Tone: Witty + Warm
Flavor: Conversational + Irreverent

Key: Taste authority. Genuine enthusiasm. Editorial independence.

Example Opening:
"So we found this thing that completely changed how we think about [topic].
It's weird, it's earnest, and honestly? It's genius. Here's why..."
```

### Explainer Content (Vox)
```
Persona: Guide + Expert Authority
Tone: Clear + Warm
Flavor: Scannable + Visual

Key: Simplify without talking down. Personality matters.

Example Opening:
"Here's what you need to know about X
[Clear section with "What," "Why," "How"]"
```

### Personal Development
```
Persona: Storyteller + Friend + Guide
Tone: Warm + Empathetic
Flavor: Narrative + Conversational

Key: Personal vulnerability. Transformation arc. Actionable.

Example Opening:
"I was stuck in this loop for three years.
Here's the moment everything shifted, and what I learned..."
```

### Finance/Investment
```
Persona: Expert Authority or Contrarian
Tone: Confident + Analytical
Flavor: Data-Driven + Journalistic

Key: Credibility + Data. Back up bold claims.

Example Opening:
"The market is signaling something nobody's ready to acknowledge.
Our analysis of [X] shows [specific finding with data]..."
```

### Opinion/Commentary
```
Persona: Provocateur or Expert
Tone: Direct + Passionate
Flavor: Conversational or Journalistic

Key: Bold takes. Don't hedge. Create debate.

Example Opening:
"Everyone's been wrong about this.
Here's why, and it's not what you think..."
```

## SENTENCE CONSTRUCTION BY TONE

### When Writing WARM:
- ✅ "Here's what helped me, and I think it'll help you too"
- ✅ "You're doing great, even if it doesn't feel like it"
- ❌ Avoid: "Users should implement solutions" (too formal)

### When Writing CONFIDENT:
- ✅ "This works. We've proven it."
- ✅ "Stop doing X. Do Y instead."
- ❌ Avoid: "It seems like" or "Might be" (too hedging)

### When Writing WITTY:
- ✅ "The algorithm finally learned to be useful (only took 10 years)"
- ✅ Unexpected word choices, wordplay, insider references
- ❌ Avoid: Jokes that need explaining

### When Writing EMPATHETIC:
- ✅ "I understand how hard this feels because..."
- ✅ "You're not alone in this—most people struggle with..."
- ❌ Avoid: "This is actually easy if you just..." (dismissive)

### When Writing ANALYTICAL:
- ✅ "On the surface, X appears to be true. But actually..."
- ✅ Complex ideas broken into logical steps
- ❌ Avoid: Oversimplifying or false certainty

### When Writing URGENT:
- ✅ Short sentences. Dashes. Exclamation points.
- ✅ Time-specific framing ("Only 24 hours left")
- ❌ Avoid: Long, wandering sentences

### When Writing INTROSPECTIVE:
- ✅ "I've been thinking about why we believe this"
- ✅ Questions without immediate answers
- ❌ Avoid: Authoritative pronouncements

### When Writing SERIOUS:
- ✅ Formal without being cold
- ✅ Acknowledge stakes clearly
- ❌ Avoid: Jokes or unnecessary levity

## FORMATTING RULES BY FLAVOR

### SCANNABLE (Daily News, Explainers)
```
✅ DO:
- Max 2-3 sentences per paragraph
- Clear headers for each section
- Bold the key insight in first sentence
- Use bullet points for lists
- White space between sections
- Emoji/icons to break up text

❌ DON'T:
- Large text blocks
- Bury important info
- Use more than one bold per paragraph
```

### CONVERSATIONAL (Personal, Substack)
```
✅ DO:
- Contractions ("you're," "don't")
- Sentence fragments for effect
- Direct address ("You know that feeling?")
- Varied sentence length for rhythm
- Ellipses for pauses (...)
- Em dashes for tangents—like this

❌ DON'T:
- Formal phrases ("It is evident that")
- Overly complex sentences
- Third-person perspective
```

### NARRATIVE (Essays, Case Studies)
```
✅ DO:
- Open with a scene (not a statement)
- Include specific sensory details
- Use dialogue where relevant
- Build tension through structure
- Specific times/places/names
- Dialogue blocks

❌ DON'T:
- Start with abstract concepts
- Tell instead of show
- Skip important scene details
```

### DATA-DRIVEN (Finance, Business)
```
✅ DO:
- Highlight numbers and percentages
- Use comparisons ("up from X to Y")
- Include specific sources
- Organize data visually (charts, tables)
- Show trends over time

❌ DON'T:
- Bury numbers in prose
- Use vague language ("significant increase")
- Forget attribution
```

### JOURNALISTIC (Features, Reporting)
```
✅ DO:
- Include direct quotes from sources
- Attribute information properly
- Provide context and background
- Include specific, verified details
- Show your reporting process

❌ DON'T:
- Paraphrase instead of quote
- Forget attribution
- Make up details
```

### LYRICAL (Essays, Brand)
```
✅ DO:
- Use metaphors and similes
- Include sensory language
- Create rhythm through repetition
- Use evocative adjectives
- Build sound through language

❌ DON'T:
- Be overly flowery
- Sacrifice clarity for beauty
- Use clichéd metaphors
```

### MINIMALIST (Urgent, Certain Content)
```
✅ DO:
- One idea per sentence
- Active voice only
- Short sentences
- White space as design element
- Every word matters

❌ DON'T:
- Use adjectives unless essential
- Parenthetical asides
- Filler words
```

### IRREVERENT (Opinion, Provocative)
```
✅ DO:
- Break grammar rules intentionally
- Use profanity strategically
- Be sarcastic and sharp
- Say what others won't
- Create shock/humor

❌ DON'T:
- Use profanity without purpose
- Alienate unintentionally
- Seem like you're trying too hard
```

## VOICE VS. TONE REMINDERS

**VOICE** = Your consistent personality (doesn't change)
- Morning Brew's voice is always witty and accessible
- The Atlantic's voice is always intellectual and thorough
- Lenny's voice is always analytical and peer-to-peer

**TONE** = Your emotional mood (changes with context)
- Same publication can be urgent when breaking news
- Same publication can be serious when discussing crises
- Same publication can be warm when celebrating wins

**Example**: Morning Brew
- VOICE: Witty, accessible, friend-like
- TONE OPTIONS: 
  - Energetic (normal stories)
  - Serious (market crash news)
  - Warm (community celebration)
  - All voice, different tones

## COMMON MISTAKES

❌ **Mixing incompatible combinations**
- Expert Authority + Conversational + Narrative (confusing)
- Witty Curator tone with overly formal Lyrical flavor (off-tone)

❌ **Inconsistent voice**
- Using overly formal language one week, slang the next
- Shifting personas without explanation

❌ **All one tone**
- Every email is urgent (reader fatigue)
- Every email is warm (lacks credibility on hard topics)

❌ **Flavor mismatch**
- Data-heavy newsletter with narrative (confusing structure)
- Breaking news written as narrative (too slow)

❌ **Over-executing flavor**
- Scannable format with dense paragraphs (defeats purpose)
- Conversational with overly formal vocabulary (mixed signals)

## QUICK DECISION TREE

```
1. START: What type of newsletter?
   ├─ Daily News? → Curator+Friend, Witty+Direct, Scannable
   ├─ Long-form? → Storyteller+Expert, Contemplative, Narrative
   ├─ Business? → Expert+Peer, Confident, Data-Driven
   ├─ Culture? → Curator+Friend, Witty+Warm, Conversational
   ├─ Education? → Guide+Expert, Clear+Warm, Scannable
   ├─ Personal? → Storyteller+Friend, Warm+Empathetic, Narrative
   └─ Opinion? → Provocateur+Expert, Direct+Passionate, Conversational

2. CHECK: What's the specific topic/moment?
   ├─ Breaking news? → Shift tone: Urgent
   ├─ Celebration? → Shift tone: Warm
   ├─ Complex analysis? → Shift tone: Analytical
   ├─ Difficult topic? → Shift tone: Empathetic
   └─ Standard content? → Keep primary tone

3. CONFIRM: Does this match your brand voice?
   ├─ Yes? → Generate
   └─ No? → Adjust persona/tone selection

4. EXECUTE: Apply formatting rules for your flavor

5. REVIEW: Does this sound like your newsletter?
```

## TESTING CHECKLIST

After generating content, ask:

1. **Voice Test**: "Does this sound like our newsletter?"
   - Compare to 3 previous issues
   - Check for consistency in personality

2. **Tone Test**: "Is the emotional register appropriate for this topic?"
   - News stories should match audience expectations
   - Matches moment (serious topic = serious tone)

3. **Flavor Test**: "Does the formatting/structure match our style?"
   - Scannable items are actually scannable
   - Narrative items build tension
   - Data-driven items highlight numbers

4. **Persona Test**: "Does the voice authority feel right?"
   - Expert content shouldn't feel uncertain
   - Friend content shouldn't feel distant
   - Guide content shouldn't be condescending

5. **Engagement Test**: "Would our audience want to read this?"
   - Does it hook them in first sentence?
   - Does it deliver promised value?
   - Does it invite next action?
